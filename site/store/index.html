The lantern store.

Curated games for the lantern engine, each one a single .lant file. Every store game is written in wick, so it is pure data — no native code, no file access, no network. The worst a game here can do is render rude pixels.

Games

To play: grab the engine from the releases page, then lantern kora-night.lant. macOS today; iPhone/iPad next.

Sell your game here

The engine is free and open source; the store is how it sustains itself — and most of every sale belongs to the person who made the game.

You keep 85%

The store takes 15% of each sale — payment processing comes out of our share, not yours. Compare: Steam takes 30%. Free games cost nothing to list. Paid checkout opens soon; the catalog and submissions are open today.

Write it in wick

Store games are wick-only — that is what makes them safe to distribute and cheap to review honestly. Lua remains fully supported for local development. Learn wick — it's Lua-small, and the compiler catches the bugs Lua saves for your players.

Package it

One command: lantern_pack mygame/ mygame.lant. Add a game.info with your id, title, and version. The format is documented and tiny — your game stays yours.

Submit it

Open a submission on GitHub with your .lant attached. Curation is a human playing your game: it must run at 60 fps, be your own work, and be finishable. That's the bar.
